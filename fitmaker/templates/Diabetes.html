Diet for Preventing Diabetes

Food Category Recommended Limit/Avoid
Vegetables Include a variety of colorful, non-starchy vegetables. Limit starchy vegetables like potatoes, corn, and peas.
Fruits Choose whole fruits over juices. Limit fruit juices and dried fruits (high in added sugars).
Whole Grains Opt for whole grains like brown rice, quinoa, and oats. Limit refined grains (white bread, white rice, sugary cereals).
Protein Choose lean sources of protein (chicken, fish, tofu, legumes). Limit red and processed meats. Watch portion sizes.
Dairy Choose low-fat or fat-free options. Limit full-fat dairy products.
Fats Include healthy fats from sources like olive oil, avocados, and nuts. Limit saturated fats and trans fats found in fried and processed foods.
Sweets and Snacks Enjoy occasional treats in moderation. Limit sugary snacks, desserts, and sugary beverages.
Beverages Drink water, herbal teas, and unsweetened beverages. Limit sugary drinks, including soda and energy drinks.

It's important to note that portion control, regular physical activity, and maintaining a healthy weight are also crucial factors in preventing diabetes. Additionally, individual dietary needs may vary, so it's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian for personalized advice based on specific health conditions and goals.
