Cholesterol Prevention Diet

Category Food Choices
Fruits and Vegetables Eat a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables. Aim for at least 5 servings per day. They are rich in antioxidants and fiber. Examples: Berries, apples, broccoli, spinach, carrots.
Whole Grains Choose whole grains over refined grains. Include oats, brown rice, quinoa, and whole wheat in your diet. They provide fiber, vitamins, and minerals.
Lean Proteins Opt for lean protein sources to replace saturated fats. Examples include skinless poultry, fish, legumes, tofu, and low-fat dairy products.
Healthy Fats Include sources of unsaturated fats, such as olive oil, avocados, nuts, and seeds. These can help improve cholesterol levels.
Fatty Fish Incorporate fatty fish like salmon, mackerel, and trout, which are high in omega-3 fatty acids. Omega-3s can help lower triglycerides and protect the heart.
Limit Saturated Fats Reduce intake of saturated fats found in red meat, full-fat dairy, and processed foods. Replace with healthier fats like olive oil or avocado.
Limit Trans Fats Avoid trans fats found in many processed and packaged foods. Check labels for partially hydrogenated oils. Trans fats can raise LDL (bad) cholesterol.
High-Fiber Foods Increase dietary fiber to help lower cholesterol levels. Choose whole fruits, vegetables, legumes, and whole grains.
Nuts and Seeds Include nuts like almonds, walnuts, and seeds such as flaxseeds and chia seeds. They are rich in healthy fats, fiber, and antioxidants.
Plant Sterols Foods fortified with plant sterols or stanols can help lower LDL cholesterol. These are found in certain margarines, orange juice, and yogurt.
Limit Added Sugars Reduce the consumption of added sugars and sugary beverages, as they can contribute to weight gain and increased triglyceride levels.
